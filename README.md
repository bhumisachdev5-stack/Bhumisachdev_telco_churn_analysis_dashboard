# 📊 Telco Customer Churn Analysis — Power BI Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes customer churn behavior for a telecommunications company using Power BI. The goal is to identify key drivers of churn and provide actionable insights that help the business reduce customer attrition.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem
Customer churn is one of the most critical challenges in the telecom industry. Losing a customer costs significantly more than retaining one. This dashboard helps stakeholders:
- Understand **who is churning** and **why**
- Identify **high-risk customer segments**
- Make data-driven decisions to improve **customer retention**

---

## 📂 Dataset
- **Source:** IBM Telco Customer Churn Dataset (publicly available)
- **Size:** ~7,000 customer records
- **Key Features:**
  - Demographics: Gender, Senior Citizen, Partner, Dependents
  - Services: Internet Service, Phone Service, Streaming, Online Security
  - Account Info: Contract Type, Payment Method, Monthly Charges, Tenure
  - Target: Churn (Yes/No)

## 📊 Dashboard Features

### Key KPIs (Cards)
- Total Customers
- Total Churned Customers
- Churn Rate (%)
- Average Monthly Charges
- Average Tenure (months)

### Visuals Included
- **Churn by Contract Type** — Bar chart showing month-to-month contracts have highest churn
- **Churn by Internet Service** — Fiber optic users churn more than DSL
- **Churn by Tenure** — Newer customers (0–12 months) are at highest risk
- **Churn by Payment Method** — Electronic check users show highest churn
- **Demographics Breakdown** — Senior citizens, customers without partners/dependents
- **Monthly Charges vs Churn** — Higher charges correlate with higher churn
- **Interactive Slicers** — Filter by gender, contract type, internet service

---

## 🔍 Key Insights
1. **Month-to-month contracts** account for the majority of churned customers (~88%)
2. **Fiber optic internet** users churn at a significantly higher rate than DSL users
3. **New customers (tenure < 12 months)** are the most vulnerable to churn
4. **Electronic check** payment users churn more than auto-pay users
5. Customers **without online security or tech support** services tend to churn more
6. **Senior citizens** show a higher churn rate compared to non-senior customers

---

## 💡 Recommendations
- Offer **incentives to switch from month-to-month to annual contracts**
- Investigate **fiber optic pricing** and service quality issues
- Launch **onboarding programs** for new customers in their first year
- Promote **auto-payment options** to reduce electronic check usage
- Bundle **security/support services** at discounted rates to improve retention
